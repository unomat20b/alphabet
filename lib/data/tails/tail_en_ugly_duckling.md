# The Ugly Duckling (Hans Christian Andersen)

It was lovely summer weather in the country, and the golden corn, the green oats, and the haystacks piled up in the meadows looked beautiful.

A duck was sitting on her nest, waiting for her young ones to come out of their shells. At last, one shell cracked, and then another, and soon all the ducklings came out and looked around them.

"Quack, quack!" said their mother. "Come along and see the big world."

But one egg was still unhatched. At last it cracked, and out came a large, awkward duckling.

The other ducklings stared at him.

"He is very big and ugly," they said. "He is not like us."

The poor duckling did not know what to do. Everywhere he went, the animals laughed at him.

"You are too ugly to be here," they said.

So he ran away and lived alone in the marsh.

The winter came. It was very cold, and the duckling almost froze in the ice.

But when spring returned, something wonderful happened.

The duckling saw some beautiful swans flying above him. He felt a strange longing to join them.

"I will go to them," he said, "even if they kill me."

But when he looked into the water, he saw his reflection — and he was no longer ugly.

He had grown into a beautiful swan.

The other swans welcomed him, and he was happier than he had ever been.

"I never dreamed of such happiness," he said, "when I was an ugly duckling."
