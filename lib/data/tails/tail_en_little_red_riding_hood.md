# Little Red Riding Hood

Once upon a time, there was a little girl who always wore a red cloak with a hood. Everyone called her Little Red Riding Hood.

One day, her mother said,
"Your grandmother is ill. Take this basket of food to her, but do not leave the path."

Little Red Riding Hood promised to be careful and set off through the forest.

As she walked along the path, she met a wolf.

"Where are you going, little girl?" asked the wolf.

"I am going to my grandmother’s house," she said. "She lives at the end of the forest."

The wolf smiled to himself and said,
"Why don’t you pick some flowers for her? I’m sure she would like that."

Little Red Riding Hood thought it was a good idea and went off the path to pick flowers.

Meanwhile, the wolf ran quickly to the grandmother’s house.

He knocked on the door.

"Who is there?" asked the grandmother.

"It is me, Little Red Riding Hood," said the wolf, trying to sound like her.

The grandmother opened the door, and the wolf jumped inside and swallowed her whole.

Then he put on her clothes, got into her bed, and waited.

After a while, Little Red Riding Hood arrived.

She knocked on the door.

"Come in," said the wolf in a strange voice.

Little Red Riding Hood entered the house and walked up to the bed.

"Grandmother, what big ears you have!" she said.

"All the better to hear you with," replied the wolf.

"And what big eyes you have!"

"All the better to see you with."

"And what big teeth you have!"

"All the better to eat you with!" shouted the wolf.

And he jumped out of bed.

Just then, a woodsman who was passing by heard the noise and rushed inside.

He chased the wolf away and saved Little Red Riding Hood and her grandmother.

From that day on, Little Red Riding Hood always remembered her mother’s advice and never left the path again.
