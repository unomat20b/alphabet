# The Gingerbread Man

Once upon a time, there was a little old woman and a little old man who lived in a small house.

One day, the old woman decided to bake a gingerbread man. She mixed the dough, rolled it out, and cut it into the shape of a little man. She added eyes, a nose, and a smiling mouth.

Then she put him into the oven to bake.

After a while, she opened the oven door — and suddenly, the gingerbread man jumped up and ran out!

"Stop!" cried the old woman. "Come back!"

But the gingerbread man just laughed and shouted:
"Run, run, as fast as you can!  
You can’t catch me, I’m the Gingerbread Man!"

He ran past the old man, who tried to catch him, but failed.

Then he ran past a cow.

"Stop! I want to eat you!" said the cow.

But the gingerbread man only laughed:
"I’ve run away from a little old woman and a little old man,  
and I can run away from you, I can!"

And he kept running.

Next, he met a horse.

"Stop! I want to eat you!" said the horse.

But again he sang:
"I’ve run away from a cow,  
and I can run away from you, I can!"

And he ran on.

Soon he came to a river. He could not swim.

Then a clever fox appeared.

"Jump on my back," said the fox. "I will carry you across the river."

The gingerbread man trusted him and jumped onto the fox’s back.

As they crossed the river, the fox said,
"You are too heavy. Climb onto my nose."

The gingerbread man climbed onto the fox’s nose.

Suddenly — snap!

The fox tossed him up and ate him in one bite.

And that was the end of the Gingerbread Man.
